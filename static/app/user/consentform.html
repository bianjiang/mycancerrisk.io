Consent Form

Title of Project: Developing a Facebook app to promote colorectal cancer screening: pilot testing a social media intervention

Principal Investigator: Yi Guo, Assistant Professor University of Florida

  • Purpose of the Study: The purpose of this study is to test a Facebook-based social media app and evaluate its effect on Colorectal Cancer knowledge, screening intentions, and screening rate. We may also ask other questions about you and your health care.
  • Procedures to be followed: The study includes 3 parts: a pre-study interview, using the app, and a post-study interview.
  • Duration: Each telephone interview will take approximately 15-20 minutes. The app will send 1 message per month over the 6-months.
  • Research Benefits: There is no direct benefit to you for being in this study. There may be a benefit to others depending on the results of this study.
  • Research Risks: When you install an app through Facebook, per Facebook’s Terms of Agreement, the app has permission to access your public profile, which includes your name, profile pictures, username, user ID (account number), networks and any info you choose to make publicly available. Facebook may update its Terms of Agreement at any time. By signing up for Facebook and agreeing to the Terms of Agreement, you acknowledge your responsibility for managing the information you share on Facebook. If you choose to share the app on Facebook, other users will be able to see that you are using the app. You are able to adjust your privacy settings for each individual app you use through Facebook, and you can delete the app at any time. Facebook will know that you are using this app. However, the App and the associated data are housed outside of Facebook as a method to protect subjects’ data. There is a risk that information received by authorized persons or agencies could then be passed on to others beyond your authorization and not covered by HIPAA.
  • Statement of Confidentiality: Your participation in this research is confidential. The survey does not ask for any information that would identify who the responses belong to. In the event of any publication or presentation resulting from the research, no personally identifiable information will be shared because your name is in no way linked to your responses.
  • Right to Ask Questions: Please contact Dr. Yi Guo at the University of Florida and you may contact him at (352) 294-5969 with questions or concerns about this study.
  • Payment for participation: Participants will receive a $10.00 visa gift card for each survey completed. There will be two surveys for a total of $20.00.
  • Privacy Authorization: UF and Shands will be allowed to collect, use and/or give out your medical information, but only to:
    • Other researchers whose research is approved by an Institutional Review Board (IRB).
    • The sponsor of a study (like a drug company), the Food and Drug Administration, the Department of Health and Human Services, the Office of Human Research Protections or other Government agencies.
  • Voluntary Participation: Your decision to be in this research is voluntary. You can stop at any time. You do not have to answer any questions you do not want to answer. If you choose not to take part, this will have no effect on your current or future health care at Shands Hospital at the University of Florida. If you have any questions about your rights as a research subject, you can phone the Institutional Review Board at 352-273-9600. If you have any questions regarding the study you can contact the PI, Yi Guo at 352-294-5969.

Use of the app implies that you have read the information in this form and consent to take part in the research. Please keep this form for your records or future reference.
